Sanctify ("we," "us," or "our") provides faith-based internet protection for families and organizations. This Privacy Policy explains what we collect, how we use it, and what choices you have.

1. Information We Collect

Account and Billing Information

When you create an account, we collect your family name, email address, subscription selections, and related billing records. Payment details are processed by Stripe and are not stored on Sanctify servers.

Device and Protection Status

For each device you register, we collect the device name, platform, and a unique device identifier. We also collect health signals such as app version, OS version, battery level, and protection status so we can tell whether filtering is active and working.

Filter and Compliance Summaries

Sanctify records category-level filter events, such as adult content, gambling, or profile tampering, so family administrators and approved shepherds can understand whether protection is working. We do not promise “zero metadata,” but we do avoid building a browsing-history product. Specific browsing history is not shown to shepherds.

Network and Abuse Signals

We collect IP addresses and related network signals to detect suspicious activity, account sharing, and service abuse. We do not use these signals for precise consumer location tracking.

Optional Analytics, Attribution, and Marketing Cookies

If you allow optional analytics and marketing cookies, we store a consent choice in your browser and collect limited site and app usage data, such as the page that led to signup, selected plan intent, whether checkout or the billing portal was opened, and whether onsite follow-up forms were shown or submitted. This helps us understand which pages, offers, and follow-up flows actually convert. If you decline, the service still works and we do not load that optional analytics and marketing layer on your device.

Marketing Leads and Follow-Up Emails

If you explicitly opt in to marketing emails on the public site, Sanctify stores your email address, your name if provided, the page where you opted in, your selected interest, and any attribution details you allowed. We use that information to send follow-up emails only until you unsubscribe or create an account.

Contact Requests

If you submit the contact form, Sanctify stores the contact details and message you send so the team can respond, route the request, and track whether follow-up happened. If you also opt in to updates, that request can create or refresh a marketing lead record for future campaign follow-up.

2. How We Use Information

  • To provide and maintain Sanctify protection and device setup
  • To detect when protection has been disabled, bypassed, or degraded
  • To send accountability or compliance alerts to family administrators and shepherds
  • To process payments and manage subscriptions
  • To enforce household and device limits
  • To detect and prevent account sharing or abuse
  • To improve the service and develop features such as Smart Filter AI
  • To send opt-in follow-up emails to visitors who asked to hear from Sanctify
  • To respond to contact requests and shepherd or organization onboarding inquiries

3. What Shepherds Can See

If your family has connected with a shepherd, such as a pastor, rabbi, imam, or other spiritual leader, they can see:

  • Your family name and number of devices
  • Device protection status, such as protected, degraded, or unprotected
  • Blocked content categories only, such as adult content or gambling
  • Compliance events, such as protection being removed or weakened

Shepherds can never see specific URLs, domain names, or browsing history. They see only aggregate category-level information to support your family's accountability goals.

4. Cookies, Browser Storage, and Privacy Choices

Sanctify uses a small browser record to remember your optional cookie choice across sanctify.faith and app.sanctify.faith. If you allow optional analytics and marketing cookies, we also store limited first-touch and last-touch attribution data so we can connect signup, billing, and consented lead activity back to the page or campaign that led to it.

You can withdraw or re-enable that optional cookie choice any time using the Privacy choices control on the site or app. Declining optional cookies does not block account access, filtering, or billing.

5. Data Retention

We retain account data for the duration of your subscription plus 30 days. Heartbeat data is retained for 90 days. Compliance events are retained for 1 year. DNS query metadata, category-level only, is retained for 30 days. When you delete your account, all personal data is removed within 30 days. You can start that request from the app settings, the web settings page, or the account deletion page.

6. Device and Household Limits

Each Sanctify account supports up to 15 devices per household. One subscription is intended for use by one household at one physical address. We monitor device registrations, heartbeat patterns, and IP addresses to detect account sharing. If abnormal usage patterns suggest an account is being shared across multiple households, we may contact you or suspend the account.

7. Children's Privacy

Sanctify is designed to be used by families, including families with children. Account registration requires a parent or guardian. We do not knowingly collect personal information directly from children under 13. All device data associated with children is managed through the family administrator's account.

8. Service Providers

We rely on service providers for parts of the platform, including:

  • Clerk for authentication and identity services
  • Stripe for payment processing
  • PostHog for optional product analytics and attribution
  • Klaviyo for optional onsite marketing forms, profile capture, and follow-up campaigns
  • AdGuard Home for filtering infrastructure
  • OpenAI for Smart Filter AI features when that add-on is enabled
  • Email delivery providers that send account and marketing messages on Sanctify's behalf

Each provider operates under its own terms and privacy commitments.

9. Security

We use encrypted connections, access controls, authentication safeguards, and operational monitoring to protect the platform. No system is perfect, but we design Sanctify to limit exposure and avoid storing more sensitive browsing detail than we need.

10. Privacy Rights and Regional Notices

Sanctify is primarily marketed in the United States, but some visitors may access the site from the EEA, UK, Switzerland, or similar jurisdictions. If those laws apply to you, you may have rights to request access, correction, deletion, portability, or objection in relation to your personal data, subject to applicable law.

Optional analytics stays off until you allow it. For privacy requests, contact us and we will route the request through the appropriate operational and legal process.

Marketing emails sent from the public site require an explicit opt-in, and every marketing email includes an unsubscribe link.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. If we make a material change, we will update this page and, when appropriate, notify active customers by email or through the app.
