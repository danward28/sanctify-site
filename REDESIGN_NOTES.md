# Sanctify Site Redesign Notes

## Review of the live page

The current WordPress homepage has clear product positioning and useful content, but the experience feels template-driven: inline styling, emoji-led cards, repeated flat sections, a small hero, and limited motion. The product screenshot is present, but it does not dominate the page the way a premium software product should.

## Redesign direction

- Lead with a large Apple-style product stage that makes the dashboard feel like the main event.
- Replace emoji cards with crisp product copy, clean hierarchy, and small animated interactions.
- Use a sticky glass navigation bar, large typography, and restrained color with teal, coral, and gold accents.
- Add an interactive faith-profile selector so the page feels personalized instead of static.
- Make the block page feel compassionate and premium, not like a browser error.
- Sharpen the Shepherd Program into a growth and recurring-revenue story.

## Second pass refinements

- Use the Sanctify script wordmark as the real navigation/footer brand, not a compressed square icon.
- Add a subtle wordmark watermark in the hero so the page feels owned by Sanctify.
- Reduce headline scale and rewrite the copy to feel calmer and less aggressive.
- Replace heavy cards with thin dividers, editorial spacing, and softer product surfaces.
- Keep the CTA visible, but make the page feel more guided than forceful.
- Replace the hand-made blocked-page mockup with the public `blockpage-demo.html` experience.
- Move the organization revenue-share message into the hero/proof area so families understand early that subscribing through their organization sends support back to that same community.

## Local prototype

Open `index.html` in a browser. The prototype is self-contained except for outbound links to Sanctify signup and the public blocked-page demo.

## Multi-page pass

- Converted the single-page prototype into a connected site shell with local pages for home, how it works, pricing, shepherds, about, contact, signup, privacy, and terms.
- Kept the script wordmark in the header and footer on every page so the brand feels consistent.
- Moved the recurring-revenue message higher on Pricing and For Shepherds, including the concrete 10% shepherd revenue share from the public terms page.
- Reused the public blocked-page demo instead of rebuilding a duplicate local block page.
- Styled legal and contact pages so they no longer feel like untouched WordPress templates.
