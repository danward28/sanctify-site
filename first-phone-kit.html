Parent pre-flight checklist

Before the phone leaves your hand.

Use this section before the first unsupervised use. A rule you cannot explain calmly will be hard to enforce consistently.

01

Open with trust.

This phone is a privilege because we trust you, and it also brings doors into places we do not want shaping your heart.

02

Name the standard.

Our family follows a faith standard. That means some things are not for us, even when other families allow them.

03

Make mistakes survivable.

If something slips through, tell us. You will not be punished for asking for help when you need it.

Conversation script

The first talk should lower shame, not raise suspicion.

Children are more likely to tell the truth when they know the first response will be help, not humiliation. The kit gives parents language for that first conversation.

  • Short enough to rememberThree clear ideas are better than a thirty-minute lecture.
  • Specific enough to enforceNighttime, app approval, blocked moments, and trusted help are all named.
  • Faith-shaped without being vagueThe standard is connected to formation, peace, and truth.

First-phone family covenant

Make the agreement visible.

I will bring confusing, tempting, or frightening content to my parent instead of hiding it.

I will not use private browsing, alternate accounts, or another device to work around our family rules.

I understand that protection is here to guard peace, trust, and formation, not to embarrass me.

When I make a mistake, I can tell the truth and receive help.

Warning signs

Watch for patterns, not just websites.

These are not proof of wrongdoing. They are signals to slow down and talk before the screen becomes a private world.

Privacy gets intense

The phone is suddenly hidden, guarded, or taken to bed when it used to be casual.

A chat becomes central

An AI companion, group chat, or online account becomes emotionally important and hard to discuss.

Fear replaces openness

Your child suddenly fears school, social media, or a specific thread, image, or anonymous contact.

What age is the First-Phone Kit for?

It is most useful before a child receives their first personal phone, but it also works when a family needs to reset expectations around a device already in use.

Is this only for Christian families?

No. The kit is written for faith-based households broadly. Sanctify supports multiple faith profiles, and the principles are meant to help any family set a clear standard with less shame.

Should I download the PDF or just use this page?

Use the page to preview the kit. Download the PDF when you want a printable checklist, covenant, and conversation script you can mark up together.

Does Sanctify install protection silently?

No. Families still need to install device protection with guided setup. Sanctify makes the process approachable and gives blocked moments a calmer next step.
