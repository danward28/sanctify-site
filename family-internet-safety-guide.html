Best use

Read one section at a time, then decide which family boundary needs to become concrete this week.

Part 1

Name the threats plainly.

Parents cannot protect against what everyone is too embarrassed or too overwhelmed to name.

AI companions

Chatbots can become confidants, romantic partners, secret counselors, or sexualized conversations. Treat them as relationship environments, not toys.

Deepfake and nudifier tools

Any ordinary photo can be misused. Children need to know that a fake image can still cause real harm, and victims should never carry the shame.

AI-powered sextortion

Scammers use automation, fake identities, and pressure to move quickly. The family rule should be simple: if someone threatens you, bring it to an adult immediately.

Part 2

Use a script that lowers shame.

The first conversation matters because it teaches whether your child should hide or ask for help.

If an AI app starts feeling like a secret relationship, tell us. We will slow down and help you sort it out.

If someone creates or sends a sexual image, even a fake one, tell us immediately. You will not be blamed for being targeted.

If someone threatens you online, do not negotiate, pay, or keep talking. Bring the phone to us and we will handle it together.

If you are tempted to keep returning to something that is hurting you, tell the truth early. The earlier we talk, the easier it is to repair.

Part 3

Watch for patterns, not just websites.

These are not proof of wrongdoing. They are signals to slow down and ask better questions.

  • A chatbot, app, or online account becomes emotionally central and private.
  • The device is hidden, guarded, or taken to bed when it used to be casual.
  • Your child suddenly fears school, social media, or a specific group chat.
  • Adult content becomes a repeated pattern rather than a one-time stumble.
  • Prayer, family time, sleep, schoolwork, or friendships start losing ground to the screen.
Part 4

Turn concern into a household standard.

A family standard works best when it is short, concrete, and repeated.
